Philosophy

It's hard to distill what I feel is important from my employment history, so I felt it useful to highlight the things I spend most of my days thinking about.

People - You can't have results without growing and retaining talent. As a senior manager overseeing multiple teams, I try to lead people by instilling a culture of quality, curiosity, accountability, and personal growth. When things don't go right, I look at myself and our processes. My leadership is most strongly correlated with the engagement of my teams.

Technology - What I live and breathe. While I have had to rethink my approach to staying on the cutting-edge when I cannot code eight hours a day, I take every opportunity to learn, teach, and experiment. I'm currently interested in making applications laser focused, easily managed, ultra-secure, and highly observable. The move to cloud services and managed platforms has been a thrilling ride.

Delivery - At the intersection of engaged employees and strong technical architecture is consistent delivery of value to customers. I know I'm not a business guru, but what I can bring is business enablement. I take pride when my teams can delight our users with the features they need, without sacrificing stability or our ability to safeguard their data.
